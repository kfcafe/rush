⚡ Performance — Before & After

How performance beans (#40, #49, #50, #51) shift Rush's numbers relative to competitors.

Cold Startup Time

Before vs After beans — Rush's -c path drops unnecessary allocations
After beans: Rush cold startup drops from 4.9ms to ~3.0ms by not allocating Corrector, SuggestionEngine, or full history in -c mode. This puts Rush ahead of Zsh and within striking distance of Bash. Daemon stays at 0.4ms.

Memory Footprint

Before vs After — lazy allocation and clone reduction
Subshell runtime clone optimization (Bean #50) avoids copying entire history + aliases. Lazy Corrector/SuggestionEngine saves ~2MB. FxHashMap reduces per-lookup overhead.

AI Agent Workloads — After Performance Beans

With reduced startup overhead and fewer clones, repeated-invocation workloads improve the most
Git Status ×100 sees the biggest gain: each invocation saves ~1.9ms of unnecessary allocation, compounding to ~190ms savings over 100 calls. Rush's 8.4x average speedup over Bash increases to ~10x.
